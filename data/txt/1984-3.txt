Faget vårt kan vel sees som en gren av den anvendte matematikk.
Det er derfor helt naturlig at utdannelsen av statistikere ved
universitetene gir mye plass til

fagets matematisk-statistiske

fundament. Det er beherskelsen av dette fundamentet som først og
fremst skiller fagstatistikerne ut som en egen profesjonsgruppe,

til forskjell fra alle dem som i større eller mindre grad kan bruke

uten å ha noen dypere forståelse

de statistiske metoder

Det er derfor, etter min mening,

avgjørende at utdannelsen

beholder sin presise matematiske karakter. Det er bare statistikere

med denne grundige utdannelse som kan bidra til å minske den omfattende misbruken av statistikken som skjer på mange felter.

Etter

disse rosende ord til de eksisterende utdannelsesformer,

vil jeg gå over til å se på det jeg oppfatter som en viktig mangel
ved dem slik de praktiseres enkelte steder. Det jeg savner er et

visst innslag av praktisk statistikk.

1985 — ASTIN COMPETITION FOR YOUNG RESEARCHERS

ASTIN (Actuarial Studies in Non-Life Insurance) is a section of the
International Actuarial Association that has as its main objective
the promotion of actuarial research, particularly in non-life

insurance.
The Committee of ASTIN has decided to initiate a competition for
Young Researchers, in which prizes will be awarded for the three
best papers in actuarial science submitted to the ASTIN Bulletin
before May Ist, 1985.

The competition is open to anyone of any nationality, whether or not

employed in the insurance industry, whether or not holding a degree
in actuarial science. The only condition is that participants should
be born after April 30, 1952.
Entries must be typewritten and may be in English or French.

Participation in the competition implies automatic submission of the
essay to the ASTIN Bulletin. Thus, it implies that the paper

contains original unpublished work and is not being submitted for
publication elsewhere. The paper should not exceed 25 typewritten
pages in length. Ph.D. theses are not acceptable.
Authors should follow the "Instructions to Authors" of the ASTIN
Bulletin, to be found on the back cover of a recent issue.
Entries must be in the hands of the Editor of ASTIN,
Mr. P.ter BERG,

Hildebrandhove 38,
1985.

2726 AW Zoetermeer, Netherlands,

not later than May Ist,

Entries must be submitted under a pseudonym. Under the same cover,
but in a separate sealed envelope, a note is to be enclosed
repeating the pseudonym and giving the name, address, occupation,
and age of the author(s).

A jury, composed of industrial and academic researchers, will select
the three best entries.
expense,

The winners will then be invited, at ASTIN's
to attend the 18th ASTIN Colloquium, to be held, in

Biarritz, France, October 6-10,1985 where the selected papers will

be presented in a special session. The final awards will be
announced at the end of that session, the jury taking into accomt
both the quality of the paper and the presentation.

If the paper is written by several authors, ASTIN will onlv meet the
travel expenses of one of the authors. In any case, winners are
expected to keep their travel expenses at a reasonable level (i.e.

by travelling on discount fares).
The three prize-winning papers will subsequently be published in a
special issue of the ASTIN Bulletin, together with other competition

entries accepted for publication.
